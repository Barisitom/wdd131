Rivers, Nigeria

Rivers State is one of the states in Nigeria, located in the Niger Delta region in the southern part of the country. It is bordered by Imo, Abia, and Anambra states to the north, AkwaIbom State to the east, and the Atlantic Ocean to the south. The capital of Rivers State is Port Harcourt, which is also the largest city in the state and a major economic hub. Nigeria, on the other hand, is a country in West Africa, bordered by Niger to the north, Chad to the northeast, Cameroon to the east, and Benin to the west. It has a diverse population of over 200 million people and is known for its rich cultural heritage, diverse landscapes, and significant economic potential, particularly in the oil and gas sector.
